A. Lego-like layout

Grid system: use consistent spacing and alignment, like a 12-column grid or a flex layout.

Padding inside blocks: every card or panel has consistent padding so it feels “contained.”

Connected blocks: sections appear attached but still visually separate with subtle contrast.

Example:

| Sidebar (light gray)| Main content (white)|
+---------------------+--------------------+
| Product Grid Cards (white)                 |
|                                            |
+---------------------+--------------------+
| Order Panel (light gray)                   |
+---------------------+--------------------+

Notice how the sidebar, main, and order panel are visually separate but aligned perfectly.

B. Contrast / color separation

Sidebar & order panel → light gray background (#F5F5F5 or #EDEDED)

Main content / product grid → white background (#FFFFFF)

Cards inside the main area → white with subtle shadows

Avoid thick borders. Let the background color and shadow do the separation.

This gives you a modern, clean, professional look.

C. Order panel improvement

Break it into sections: items list, discounts, totals, payment buttons

Add small card blocks inside the panel for each item (rounded corners + soft shadow)

Keep consistent spacing

Use contrast between background of panel vs cards vs buttons

Example:

Order Panel (light gray)
 ├ Items Card (white, shadow)
 ├ Discounts Card (white, shadow)
 ├ Total Card (white, shadow)
